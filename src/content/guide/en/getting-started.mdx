# Getting started

This guide walks you through setting up a brand-new clinic on Cradlen and taking
it all the way to a booked, examined, and paid visit. Follow the steps in order —
a couple of them depend on earlier ones being done first.

<Callout type="tip" title="The short version">
Register → create your organization → review settings → add staff → add services
and a default price list → book a visit → run the visit → take payment.
</Callout>

## Set up your clinic, step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign up at **/sign-up** with your name, email, phone, and a password, then
    confirm your email. This creates the personal login you'll use to manage the
    clinic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your organization and first branch">
    On **/create-organization** you name the organization, create its first
    branch, and set your owner profile (including your specialty if you practice).
    Cradlen creates the organization, the branch, and your owner profile, then
    drops you into the dashboard for that branch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review settings">
    Open **Settings** from the sidebar. Confirm the organization profile, the
    branch details and the specialties it serves, and your subscription plan —
    the plan sets your limits for staff, visits, and invoices. You can add more
    branches here at any time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your staff">
    Go to **Staff** and invite team members by email. For each person assign a
    role (owner, staff, or branch manager) and a job function (doctor,
    receptionist, or accountant). For staff with the doctor job function, set the
    specialty and subspecialties and a weekly schedule (the working days and
    hours). Invited staff accept from the email link and appear on the team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add services and a default price list">
    In **Financial → Services**, build your service catalog (consultations,
    procedures, lab tests, imaging, and so on). Then create one **price list and
    mark it as the default** — bookings and invoices read their prices from it.
    Finally, on the **Authorizations** tab, authorize each doctor for the
    services they deliver. You can layer per-doctor or per-branch price overrides
    later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Book a visit">
    From **Visits**, open the booking drawer and choose specialty → doctor →
    subspecialty, select or create the patient inline, pick a service, and set
    the date and time. Submitting creates the visit and a draft charge for the
    booked service.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the visit and examination">
    Open the visit to load its examination workspace. The form adapts to the
    visit's subspecialty. The doctor records the exam, adds any extra charges as
    care is delivered, and marks the visit completed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bill the visit and take payment">
    In **Financial → Invoices**, turn the visit's pending charges into an invoice,
    then record the payment (cash, card, and so on). The invoice moves from draft
    to issued to paid as money comes in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Before you can book

<Callout type="warn" title="Services and a default price list come first">
Booking looks up a price when you pick a service, so your service catalog and a
**default price list** must exist before you book your first visit.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warn" title="Doctors need a specialty and a schedule">
Booking filters doctors by specialty and checks availability against their
schedule. Add at least one doctor with a specialty and an active weekly schedule
before booking.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warn" title="Doctors must be authorized for their services">
A doctor can only be booked for a service they're authorized to deliver.
Authorize each doctor for their services under **Financial → Services →
Authorizations** before booking.
</Callout>
