# Roles & permissions

Access in Cradlen is decided by two things working together: a person's **role**
and their **job function**. Understanding the difference explains why two people
with the same title can see different screens.

## Roles vs. job functions

- **Role** is the kind of account: **owner**, **doctor**, or **reception**. It
  sets the broad shape of what someone can do.
- **Job function** describes what they actually do day to day — clinical (a
  practitioner) or non-clinical (administrative, financial). It refines access
  on top of the role.

This pairing produces the personas the app recognizes — for example an *owner who
is also a doctor* sees clinical workspaces, while an *owner who doesn't practice*
does not.

<Callout type="note" title="Why a clinical job function matters">
Clinical surfaces — examination workspaces and patient clinical detail — are
gated on a clinical job function, not just the role. An owner without one manages
the clinic but won't open exam screens.
</Callout>

## What each persona focuses on

- **Owner** — full clinic setup: organization and branch settings, staff, the
  service catalog and pricing, and reports. Clinical screens only with a clinical
  job function.
- **Doctor** — the clinical workspace: their schedule, visits, examinations, and
  patient history.
- **Reception** — day-to-day operations: booking visits, checking patients in,
  creating invoices, and collecting payment.
- **Accountant** *(non-clinical financial)* — invoices, payments, cash sessions,
  and financial reports.
- **Branch manager** — operational oversight of a branch; clinical access again
  depends on whether they hold a clinical job function.

## How this shows up in the app

The sidebar only lists what your persona can reach — there are no dead links to
screens you can't open. The same permission rules are enforced again on the
server, so the menu is a convenience, not the security boundary.

<Callout type="tip" title="Setting roles">
Assign roles and job functions when you invite or edit a team member under
**Staff**. See **[Getting started](/guide/getting-started)** for the invite flow.
</Callout>
