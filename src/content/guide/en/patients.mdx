# Patients

The **Patients** page is your branch's patient directory — search the people on
file, see their journey status at a glance, and (for clinicians) open a patient's
full clinical workspace.

## The patient list

Each row shows the patient's **name**, **phone**, **address**, **last visit**,
their **journey** type, and a **status** badge. You can:

- **Search** by name or contact details.
- **Filter** by journey status: All, Active, Completed, or Cancelled.
- **Switch scope** between **This branch** (the default) and **All patients**
  (org-wide — available to owners).

Doctors see **their own** patients by default; owners and branch managers see the
whole branch directory and a few summary stats.

## Opening a patient

A patient row responds to two interactions:

- **Single-click** — selects (highlights) the row.
- **Double-click** — opens that patient's **clinical workspace**.

<Callout type="note" title="Opening the workspace is for clinicians">
Only staff with the **doctor** job function can open a patient's record, so
double-click opens the workspace for them. Reception and non-clinical owners or
branch managers still see the patient list, but double-clicking a row does
nothing — opening a clinical record requires a clinical job function. See
**[Roles & permissions](/guide/roles-and-permissions)**.
</Callout>

## Inside the patient workspace

The workspace has two tabs:

- **Overview** — the patient's demographics and attachments, a timeline of past
  visits, and — for OB/GYN — the OB/GYN history, current pregnancy journey, and
  vitals charts.
- **History** — the specialty medical history.

Owners and branch managers also get an **Edit profile** action to update
demographics (name, date of birth, phone, address). The **national ID is locked**
by default and takes an explicit confirmation to change.

## Adding patients

There's no "add patient" button here — patients are created during the **booking
flow**, where reception can add a new patient inline while booking a visit. See
**[Visits](/guide/visits)**.
