# Medicines

The **Medicines** page is your clinic's medication catalog — the list of drugs
your team maintains and prescribes from during visits. Keeping it tidy means
faster, more consistent prescriptions.

## The medicine list

Each medicine shows its **name**, **form**, **strength**, **category**, a
**default dose** (amount · frequency · route), how often it's been prescribed
(**usage**), and any **notes**. To find one quickly you can:

- **Search** by name or code.
- **Filter** by category or form.
- **Sort** by name (A–Z or Z–A) or by **most prescribed**.

## Adding a medicine

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open New Medicine">
    Click **New Medicine** to open the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the essentials">
    Give it a unique **code** and a **name** — both required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add details">
    Optionally set the generic name, form, strength, category, and manufacturer
    (company).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a default dose">
    Add a default amount, unit, frequency, and route so prescriptions pre-fill
    sensibly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Add any notes (and optionally link a medical rep), then save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Edit a medicine with the **pencil** on its row, or remove it with the **trash**
icon (you'll be asked to confirm).

## Prescribing from the catalog

When a doctor prescribes a medicine on a visit, it's drawn from this catalog and
its **default dose pre-fills** — they can still adjust it for the patient. Each
medicine also tracks its **usage** (how many times it's been prescribed), which
powers the "most prescribed" sort. See **[Visits](/guide/visits)** for the
examination workflow.

## Who can manage it

<Callout type="note" title="Medicines is for clinicians and managers">
Owners, branch managers, and doctors can view and manage the catalog — add, edit,
and remove medicines. Reception and accountants don't see the Medicines page. See
**[Roles & permissions](/guide/roles-and-permissions)**.
</Callout>
